## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are prospective corporate flight clients, executive travelers, business decision-makers, assistants, and partners evaluating Eisenflügel Aviation for transportation needs at national and international scale.

They will typically land on the site with low patience and high expectations. The site must let them understand who the company is, what it does, and whether it is credible, refined, and worth contacting. This is an informational website centered on the company manifesto, not a dense product catalog or transactional booking flow.

The main job to be done is simple: establish brand trust quickly, communicate the Eisenflügel Aviation point of view with clarity, and direct serious inquiries to `contact@eisenflugel.com`.

### Brand Personality
Brand personality: Aesthetics, Ethics, Order.

The voice should feel controlled, intelligent, and premium. The emotional goals are confidence and exclusivity, without arrogance or visual excess. Messaging should read as deliberate and composed rather than promotional or overloaded.

### Aesthetic Direction
Visual direction is minimal and luxurious, grounded in the existing monochrome logo and a black-and-white primary palette. Interfaces should preserve strong order, generous whitespace, disciplined typography, and a calm editorial rhythm.

Reference qualities to borrow:
- `embryriddle.com`: clarity, institutional trust, aviation credibility
- `gulfstream.com`: premium restraint, spacious presentation, polished product storytelling
- `rolls-royce.com`: luxury cues, confidence, precision, and composure

Anti-direction:
- never feel overcrowded
- avoid overwhelmed information density
- avoid noisy marketing tropes, generic startup aesthetics, and excessive decorative effects

Support both light and dark mode, but keep the visual identity primarily monochrome. Any accent usage should remain sparse and subordinate to the black-and-white system.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with clarity. Users must understand who Eisenflügel Aviation is and what it does within seconds of landing.
2. Express luxury through restraint. Use space, hierarchy, and typography to signal exclusivity instead of ornament or visual clutter.
3. Preserve order in every layout. Grids, alignment, spacing, and sequencing should feel exacting and calm.
4. Keep content intentionally sparse. Prefer fewer, stronger statements over long blocks of information or overloaded sections.
5. Build trust through polish and accessibility. Maintain solid semantic structure, readable contrast, keyboard usability, and sensible motion without turning the site into an accessibility-heavy artifact.
